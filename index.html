Introduction

APJ Abdul Kalam was one of the most influential and respected figures in India’s history. He was a scientist, a visionary, a leader, and a role model for millions of Indians. He played a key role in developing India’s space and missile programs, and served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was known as the ‘Missile Man of India’ for his contributions to India’s defense capabilities. He also inspired the youth and the masses with his vision of transforming India into a developed nation by 2020. He was a humble and generous human being who lived a simple and spiritual life. He died on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong. He is remembered as a great patriot and a legend who made India proud.

Career

  • He joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) in 1958 as a senior scientific assistant and worked on designing hovercrafts and helicopters
  • He moved to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) in 1969 as the project director of India’s first satellite launch vehicle (SLV-3), which successfully launched Rohini satellite into orbit in 1980.
  • He returned to DRDO in 1982 as the chief of the Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme (IGMDP), which developed India’s first ballistic missiles such as Prithvi and Agni.
  • He supervised the Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998 that made India a nuclear-weapon state and earned him the nickname 'Missile Man of India".
  • He was elected as the 11th President of India in 2002 with the support of both the ruling and opposition parties, and served till 2007.
